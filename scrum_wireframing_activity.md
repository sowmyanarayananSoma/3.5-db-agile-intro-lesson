# ğŸŒ€ 1. Agile Fundamentals

Agile is a **flexible, iterative, and feedback-driven** approach to building software.

## â­ Core Principles of Agile

* Deliver software in **small usable increments**.
* **Embrace change** instead of resisting it.
* Work closely with the customer.
* Collaborate daily within the team.
* Maintain a sustainable pace.
* Continuous reflection and improvement.
* Working software is the **primary measure of progress**.

## â­ Why Agile?

Because requirements, markets, users, and technologies change constantly. Agile helps teams:

* Adapt fast
* Reduce wasted work
* Deliver earlier
* Receive feedback sooner

# ğŸ§­ 2. What Is Scrum?

Scrum is a **lightweight framework** used to apply Agile principles. It structures teamwork into **sprints**, with defined roles, meetings, and artifacts.

Scrum is NOT a methodology. It is a **framework** for continuous inspection and adaptation.

# ğŸ§‘â€ğŸ¤â€ğŸ§‘ 3. Scrum Roles

Scrum defines **three** roles.

### **Product Owner (PO)**

* Owns the **product vision**.
* Manages and prioritizes the **Product Backlog**.
* Ensures the team builds the **right thing**.

### **Scrum Master (SM)**

* Ensures Scrum is understood and followed.
* Removes blockers.
* Facilitates meetings.
* Improves team workflow.

### **Developers (The Dev Team)**

* Build the product.
* Estimate work.
* Create design, code, tests.
* Collaborate daily.

# ğŸ“… 4. Scrum Ceremonies (Meetings)

These are **timeboxed events** that keep the team aligned.

### **1. Sprint Planning**

* Team selects which user stories go into the sprint.
* Defines the sprint goal.
* Breaks down work when needed.

### **2. Daily Scrum (Daily Stand-up)**

15-minute meeting where developers share:

* What they did yesterday
* What they will do today
* What is blocking them

### **3. Sprint Review**

* Team shows completed work to stakeholders.
* Get immediate feedback.

### **4. Sprint Retrospective**

* Team reflects on what went well and what didnâ€™t.
* Creates action items for improvement.
